MEMORANDUM OF ASSOCIATION

Committee of Action for Research, Extension and Services

A Society Formed and Registered Under the Act, 1860

(Act. XXI of 1860)

MEMORANDUM OF ASSOCIATION
1. The name of the society shall be the Committee of Action for Research, Extension and Services hereinafter referred to as cares. It is a non-government, non-political, not- for- profit, charitable organization.
2. It is essentially a membership based organization. There are four categories of members: a) Founding (initially with a date-line), b) Primary, c) Institutional, d) Honorary; and a College of Fellow
3. a.) The registered office of this society shall be in Dhaka, Bangladesh.
3. b.) The society shall enjoy all right and authority on financial matters under its own seal.
3. c.) The society may open branches or other offices and subsidiaries or support groups and institutions in any locations or region in or outside of Bangladesh as per rules governing each specific situation.
3. d.) Such branches or other offices and subsidiaries, and chapters or support groups in Bangladesh or oversees shall have the authority to operate bank and other loan or depository accounts in their own locality or elsewhere, in any currency in their own name and or in the name of the society as approved by the Council/ Executive Committee in compliance with the standard rules of Bangladesh or foreign country concerned.
4. The principal objective of the society is to support innovations (research and experimentation), prototyping, and evidence based decision making together with rigorous evaluation of policy to performance to accelerate an integrated economic development and social well being of people of Bangladesh.
5. The society is conceived as a "virtual University" working together with institutions, organizations and programs for human and economic development of the country in broad field as
I. Agricultural development including fishery, livestock, horticulture, etc
II. Life sciences: Basic hygiene, preventive measures and curative measures against diseases on life;
III. Small & Medium Enterprises in agriculture, food processing and promotion of agro-based industries, etc;
IV. Green Technology: Food Production technology for Food Security, Renewable Energy, Industrial Waste Disposal, Forestry, Climate Change, etc;
V. Rural Enterprises: Upgrading indigenous technology to produce for domestic markets, exports, etc.
5 (a) These are not inclusive, other fields may be added, like society:
VI. Shall develop promote, foster and liaison /internationally competitive research capacity in the country through pulling of resources, expertise and inter disciplinary institutional collaboration;
VII. Help establish a number of "center of excellence" at different academic and research institution that may serve as regional foci for research and training of young scientists;
VIII. Support development of a network of Centers of Excellence with complementary expertise and laboratory facilities to help meet the national scientific and related economic development goals and priorities;
IX. Work closely with the University Grants Commission (UGC), and its successor: Commission on Higher Education Bangladesh Academy of Sciences(BAS); and relevant government ministries, professional organizations and international agencies and foundation to promote and ensure excellence in higher education and enhance opportunities for young scholars;
X. Raise funds and donation and liaison grants to support research and Intuitional development; and work closely with academia, government, industries and the private sector to achieve the aims of the organization;
XI. Accept endowments, gifts, donations, grants, others funds, payments for services and to earn an income;
XII. Establish, administer and manage endowments, foundations fund and others benefactions;
XIII. Promote "research in development" in industry and liaison university-farm-industry tripartite relationship so that fruits of innovations and evidence-based experiences may optimize economic output/ public well-beings.
5. (b) Further the society may:
XIV. Establish, operate, maintain institutions and centers for training and research and promote knowledge in physical and natural sciences, technology, fine arts, liberal arts, information and communication technology, engineering sciences, architecture, business and management sciences, social sciences, nursing, medical and health sciences, literature, language and linguistic, law, agriculture, hospitality and any other subjects as it would so deem;
XV. Encourage and conduct rigorous scholarly research, investigation, invention and innovations in addition to the creation of indigenous knowledge and publish them in referred journals and other forms of scholarly media;
XVI. To establish and maintain contact with scholars and their work on the studies through collaborative studies, seminars, exchange of visits or otherwise;
XVII. Provide facilities and to foster, encourage and secure and maintain good and close relationships and amity within and among communities and peoples of Bangladesh and other countries of the world through sharing of education, scientific, social and cultural activities and exchanges;
XVIII. To carryout medical research by establishing and maintaining well equipped biological laboratories by engaging in research and development of all fields of medical sciences and in the therapies of medical treatment so as to afford medical support in a better way and to provide research facilities for carrying on research both basic and applied in all systems and discipline of medical and surgical knowledge, keeping in view the socio medical and socio economic needs of the afflicted community; and to set up colleges and chain of colleges in Bangladesh and abroad, setting up of medical training institutions; nursing colleges, offering diploma courses, to medical and para-medical students and setting up of laboratories; and purchase, acquire any equipment and instruments required for carrying out medical research; and to educate and train medical students, nurses midwives and hospital administrators;
XIX. To ensure the rights and opportunities of Bangladesh scientific personnel to participate in the programme and activities of the CARES;
XX. Undertake a systematic staff development program of its own.
XXI. To establish libraries, reading room, study centre for developing the habit of study for the benefit of the society;
XXII. To create awareness and undertake motivational work amongst public about medical sciences, public health, art and literature;
XXIII. To develop relation among the development agencies working in Bangladesh
XXIV. Institute fellowship and financial grants to scholars for further studies. (Studies including attendances at professions meetings etc.)
XXV. To create within itself, from time to time, branches, divisions, sections and other unit for proper and efficient conduct of the activities of the Center in different fields of studies.
XXVI. To set up, acquire, purchase, manage and dispose of commercial interest, ventures, properties, all types of consultancy firms, and other concerns for the benefits of society and/or its affiliated bodies.
XXVII. To arrange grants and funds from institutions, individuals, foreign donor agencies or any other governmental agencies of foreign or Bangladesh origin, any other legal organization of foreign and Bangladeshi origin or any other legal sources that are required for the purposes of society with securities or upon such securities as may be determined by the Council or the Executive Committee
XXVIII. To make amends, add, alter, vary or delete any of the objective clauses to suit the purpose of society, by observing due legal formalities thereof, from time to time, if and when so required.
XXIX. The objectives set forth herein shall be deemed as separate and independent objectives of the society except where the context otherwise expressly required and shall in no way be limited or restricted by reference to or inference from the terms of any of the sub-clauses or by the name the society.
XXX. To take such others actions as may further the aims and objectives of the Center.
6. Organization and Governance :
a) Society is envisaged as an 'apex development organization' with members and constituents as defined in the Constitution (i.e. the Articles of Association), In order to accommodate all relevant interest to guide this organization there shall be a Council of Trustees, constituted of three tires of membership : a) Founding (with a date-line), b)Primary, c) Institutional, d) Honorary, and a College of Fellows- all elected within their respective groups in the ratio of 3:2:2:3 with a total voting members of 30 (thirty) only (excluding the Honorary member)
(b) The Governance: The governance of society shall be carried by two bodies:
i) The Executive committee constituted of 15 to 17 member including non resident members (NRB) and head of four (4) substantive working committees; and the heads of statutory bodies appointment by the Council of Trustees.
ii) The Council of Trustees is a body of 30(thirty) member. Except the elected "Founder" members that would have permanent status, the tenure of others for two years and eligibility of maximum two terms.
iii) The Council shall elect a Chairman from among its peers.
iv) The Council shall appoint Advisory Committees of which at least three are statutory:
a) An International Scientific Advisory Committee, and
b) Scientific and Research Coordinate Committee
c) Policy and project Review Committee
The first would function primarily at the national level and a liaison and lobby on behalf of the CARES. The second shall concern with economic development values of different research projects and function as advisory to national research strategy development, promote further research on economic and welfare consequence of the society. The last would function as nominating/ recommending body for the projects to be submitted for funding under different auspices.
v) The council may appoint working committees each concerned with one of the substantive areas.
vi) The Chairman of Advisory Committees and heads of working Groups shall be members of the Executive Committee.
7. The management of society: A core staff under direction and guidance of a Director General/Chief Executive Officer who will be an ex-officio member of the Executive committee.
8. Sources of Funds:
a) Subscription, contribution and/or donations by Founding Members
b) Membership fees and/or donations from other types of members
c) Donations, contributions and grants by individuals and private or public organizations
d) Loans from any types of members and institution
e) Donations, contributions and/or grants from foreign individuals and institutions as per clauses of "Foreign Donation (Voluntary Activities) Regulation Ordinance 1978" of Bangladesh.
f) Any other legal sources
9. Income: The income and property of the CARES shall be applied solely towards the promotion of the objectives of the CARES as set forth herein above and no portion thereof shall be paid or transferred directly or indirectly by way of dividend, bonus or otherwise howsoever as profit to the members of the CARES
10. Dissolution: The winding up of the Society can take place by a minimum of 2/3rd (two-thirds) votes of the members of the Council upon the recommendation of the Executive committee (by 3/4th majority, at a meeting explicitly called for the purpose). The notice for winding up has to be issued minimum 30 (thirty) days before the Annual General Meeting of the Council.
If upon winding up or dissolution of the Society there remains after satisfaction of all debts and liabilities, any assets, income or property whatsoever the same shall not be paid to or distributed amongst the Members of the CARES but shall be given or transferred to some other society, institution or association having objectives similar to the objectives of the Society
