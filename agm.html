Draft Report on the first rendezvous/Annual General Meeting of
Fellows and Members of CARES held on
18 January, 2014 at the BRAC Centre

Fellows and Members (Founding Members, Primary Members including members nominated by institutions) had a get-together (rendezvous) from 11: 30 a.m. on Saturday, 18 January, 2014. at The BRAC Centre Auditorium which concluded with LUNCH. The meeting was the first rendezvous/ get-together involving all categories of persons associated with CARES which was chaired by the Dr. A. Majeed Khan. The meeting was attended by 3 Fellows, 47 Founding Members, 6 Primary Members, 1 guest. Full list of participants is appended.

2. At the outset, Dr. Khan welcomed all participants and expressed his happiness to see the presence of all those “who care for CARES” who were agents to consecrate and advance the visions of CARES. He gave a brief status of the social and economic divide in Bangladesh and how rural residents and farmers are marginalized in our society. He added that there was crisis in the country, but if we care, together we could do some good for the society and farmers. He stated that CARES was conceptualized some four years back, it had been nearly two years since the organization was registered and some eight months had passed since we announced ourselves by holding the International Conference on Biotechnology on 25-26 May, 2013. He mentioned that the organization belonged to the members and it would grow in the direction as the members would want it to be. He thus emphasized on the importance of this first large gathering involving all categories of CARES stakeholders: Fellows, Founding Members, Primary Members and the two Institutional Fellows. Dr. Khan further added that all had gathered here to hear views for trying to homogenize the society and for sharing material values.

3. The get-together then turned itself to a General Meeting, the very first of the Society , by consensus. Dr. Khan then raised the issue of consideration of nomination/election of the Council of Trustees as per article 35 of the CARES Constitution, taking the advantage of the rendezvous as a full body meeting. He explained that the CARES constitution provides for a Council of 30 members in specified proportion from the four categories of members, the organization is at an initial stage where the number of Fellows, Institutional and Primary Members are not enough and still remained on the lower side. Therefore, he proposed to turn the present number of the four categories of members, as of 18 January, 2014 to constitute a Board of Trustees until further consideration. The proposal was seconded by Dr. K. M. S. Aziz. The proposal was approved unanimously in the meeting and a Council of Trustees was formed with all present. It was further decided that no cap on the enrollment of Founding Members would be put in place until the year end, i.e. 2014 when hopefully there will be sufficient number of members in all four categories. The meeting unanimously approved that the required quorum for meetings of the Board of Trustees would be 25% of the total.

Election of the Council of Trustees

4. Following the election, the Council of Trustees adopted the following agenda for the meeting:

1. Adoption of the Annual Report,
2. Annual budget -2014,
3. Audited Accounts-2011, 2012,
4. Nomination/election of the Fellows,
5. Election of the members of the Executive Committee ,
6. Announcement of the memberships of the five working groups as proposed by respective Conveners,
7. Prospects and possibilities of fund raising, and,
8. Review and adoption of any amendment of the Constitution

Agenda Discussion Decision
Annual Report: Dr. Khan introduced the report in a nutshell and invited comments. Mr. Mohiuddin Ahmed commented on the report, in particular, reference to the linkage with the universities. He invited attention to the state of research in the country and to its unsatisfactory quality. He urged CARES members to address research issues and seek higher quality standards. He further stated that he had raised this with various institutional heads too so that all concerned work for assuring quality in research. Dr. M. Shamsher Ali and Dr. K.M.S. Aziz stated that CARES was committed to promotion of research and has put high standard as benchmark for research. Dr. Salehuddin Ahmed elaborated on the difference between action research and basic research. He further suggested that CARES research projects in the future should be of more substantive in nature. Dr. Firdausi Qadri emphasized on the following points: research had to be well planned, given due facilities, subjected to surveillance, reports to be ethically correct, duly reviewed and well written. Dr. Aziz stated that CARES Projects Review Committee and peer reviews would be able to take care of such concerns. He also named the members of the Project Review Committee, of which he is the Convener. The members are: Professor Zeba I. Seraj, Dr. Francisco Javier Zapata Arias, Dr. Md. Abdur Razzaque and two more persons to be named later. The report was considered and adopted
The annual budget: Chairman invited members to consider the annual budget. View expressed by Dr. Salehuddin Ahmed, Mr. Dewan Sadek Afzal and others was that the annual budget had to be considered after the audited accounts. It was therefore taken up in that order. Following discussion and the decision on audited accounts, the budget for 2014 was discussed. Treasurer Commodore Rahman presented the budget, a deficit one, giving an account of expected income/earnings and head-wise anticipated expenditure. Mr. Sadek Afzal noted that spending about 33% of the budget on research and projects and the rest on establishment was not right. Dr. Salehuddin Ahmed expressed similar view. Commodore Rahman stated that CARES only has a skeleton staff and expenditure on projects and research will grow when proposals and funds are obtained, when establishment cost would not grow concomitantly. Dr. Razzak stated that more internal visits should be undertaken, personnel cost had to be kept at minimum, more spending on research to take place and capital asset use rules be made and followed. Lt. Gen. M. Harun-Ar-Rashid sought a clarification about whether existing members were to contribute again towards CARES fund as Tk. 2.5 lakhs had been estimated as projected membership fee: clarification was given that this would come from new membership fees. Mr. Ziaur Rahman stated that the Islami Bank Bangladesh Ltd. had committed fund for three years, the last year being 2014, and now fund sources need to be diversified. He added that each member could approach two companies to raise fund for CARES. The decision was to note it and place for approval on 1st February, 2014 in the EGM.
Audited Account: When audited accounts for 2011 and 2012were put up for consideration, Salehuddin Ahmed, Mr. Dewan Sadek expressed view that like any other AGM, such report had to be presented in the presence of a representative of the audit firm who should be in hand to answer questions about the reports. When Chairman mentioned that such representative was not present in the meeting, but the auditing firm, Chartered Accountants, had recorded its observation in the report, the Treasurer could present reports while Accountant of CARES may answer the queries. Dr. Ahmed, Mr. Afzal and a few others, however, could not agree with the proposal. They also wanted that the audited report should also contain “Letter to the management”, which was not there in the circulated report. A discussion took place about the procedure and the rating/prominence of the audit firm. Mr. Afzal proposed that only top three audit firms of the country be considered for auditing CARES accounts. Dr. Zafrullah Choudhury drew an analogy that one should not seek only topmost physicians and that young but good doctor should also be given opportunity to practice medicine too! One member stated that considering the small size of accounts of CARES, assigning such top firms would not be practical. The general consensus was that submission of audited report without the presence of a representative of the Audit firm would not be regular. It was decided to consider this issue in the presence of a representative of the Audit firm in an Extraordinary General Meeting to be convened at 11: 30 a.m. on 1st February, 2014
Nomination/election of the Fellows: The Chairman opened the floor for discussion about inclusion of new Fellows and invited names. A few names were suggested. Maj. Gen. Dr. A.S. M. Matiur Rahman stated that CARES was still at its infancy and that discussing names for Fellowship in this manner could lead to compartmentalization. He suggested that we were at present comfortable with having 5 Fellows and let maturity come in the organization when further expansion is considered based on eminence and field of expertise. Dr. Aziz stated that we have a constitutional provision to have a maximum of 27 Fellows at any time and each Fellow was to serve for two maximum terms of 3 year each. Mr. Afzal suggested for a process to be followed and not to discuss names in such meetings. Mr. Mohiuddin Ahmed suggested that the procedure of Bangla Academy and the Asiatic Society be seen. Chairman stated that CARES had seen those models; Executive Committee had considered names in the previous occasion, but the present AGM being the highest body, we may here more names for consideration, he added. It was decided to keep the Fellowship at the current level until such time this issue is revisited.

5. Dr. Khan informed the meeting that a three year-rolling plan involving an outlay of Tk. 12 crores was at the final stage of final drafting. The plan incorporates research and other functional activities of CARES. He expressed the hope that with the full support of CARES family, the required fund would be raised.



6. Agenda item 6, 7, and 8 were not discussed, though composition of the Project Review Committee was already been announced while discussing agenda 1.The meeting ended with a vote of thanks to and from the Chair and followed by lunch.
